The Magnuson Act of 1943 was monumental in breaking the barrier of East-Asian immigration to the United States. Pushed forward by the Sino-American alliance during WWII, the legislation repealed the Chinese Exclusion Act and later led to the Immigration and Nationality Act of 1965, breaking down barriers of discriminatory foreign immigration in the U.S.
